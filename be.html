Back end development is where it all started for me, I started a VB course in college aged 16 and never looked back. One of my first projects was a C# console application that was a copy of Conways Game Of Life algorith, where a user could add "cells" too a grid and they would multiply or die depending on there surrounding squares. I finished my course specialisng in C# and then took that knowledge onto a .NET based apprenticeship course where i moved to London and started working and studying full time in Canary Wharf. I chose my placement based on the amount of C# code in the companys stack and i thought it would be easier for me to get to grips with a large application for the first time.

During my time as a back end developer I have worked on large .NET projects that have been linked with SQL and NoSQL databases, and both with a modern Javascript Front End where objects were passed between all layers. I have learnt about Encapsulation, Inheritence, polymorphism and the SOLID principles which I try my best to follow at all times, I promise.
